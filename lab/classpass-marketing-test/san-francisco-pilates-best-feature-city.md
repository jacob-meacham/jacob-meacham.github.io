# The best pilates studios in San Francisco

San Francisco's pilates scene stretches from reformer-focused boutiques in Pacific Heights to heated mat studios along the coast, and the ClassPass community has strong opinions about where to go. We analyzed thousands of reviews across dozens of studios to find where the Bay Area's best pilates experiences happen -- from high-intensity hot pilates and full-body sculpt classes to intimate reformer sessions and beginner-friendly mat work.

Studios were selected based on review volume, average rating, and consistency of positive feedback from the ClassPass community.

## 1. BODYROK -- ROK YOUR BODY SCULPT

BODYROK has become a pilates institution across the Bay Area, and its Oakland location is one of the highest-rated pilates experiences in San Francisco. The studio's signature reformer-based sculpt classes blend high-intensity pilates with music-driven energy, and reviewers consistently praise the welcoming atmosphere and instructors who go the extra mile -- especially for first-timers.

> "My first time here and the vibe was welcoming. The instructor was encouraging and warm and took extra time to get me set up before class. Great challenging class."

> "Lauren is an incredible instructor. She makes class really fun, her cues are clear and easy to follow, and she has such great energy."

**Rating:** 4.87 (500+ reviews)

## 2. Hot Yoga Plus -- Inferno Hot Pilates

If you are looking for a pilates class that will leave you drenched and exhilarated, Hot Yoga Plus in the Seacliff neighborhood delivers. Their Inferno Hot Pilates class combines high-intensity interval training with pilates principles in a heated room, creating a zero-impact, full-body experience that reviewers call addictive.

> "Pretty amazing! You sweat so much. Stay hydrated. Bring a towel and your A game. This is my weekly class."

> "A great experience! Very beginner-friendly, and I received a lot of attention and helpful guidance from instructor Kito."

**Rating:** 4.89 (500+ reviews)

## 3. NOW Power Yoga -- Hot Pilates

Corte Madera's NOW Power Yoga offers one of the most beloved hot pilates classes in the San Francisco metro area. Reviewers are effusive about instructor Julie, whose combination of clear guidance, infectious energy, and thoughtfully curated playlists has earned a devoted following.

> "Julie is terrific -- the best hot pilates class I've taken. She's equal parts inspiring, helpful, supportive, and fun, and her playlist always delivers."

> "Marcela's classes are vigorous sessions that keep me pushing further. Strong, powerful flows that make you sweat up a storm."

**Rating:** 4.90 (500+ reviews)

## 4. Mighty Pilates -- Stretch Re:Store

Tucked into Pacific Heights, Mighty Pilates is the kind of studio where classical pilates technique meets thoughtful, creative programming. Their Stretch Re:Store class has earned a loyal following among reviewers who appreciate the intentional sequencing and deep attention to form. Instructors here are known for getting the most out of the reformer with deliberate, challenging movements.

> "Loved Derrick's class! The flow was very intentional, and I appreciated when he specified which muscle group you're focusing on for each movement."

> "Rebecca was really instructive and helpful for beginners. The class was slow-paced yet challenging with the slow burn -- perfect for those interested in trying reformer pilates."

**Rating:** 4.82 (500+ reviews)

## 5. BODYROK Berkeley -- SIX PACKS & BACKS

BODYROK's Berkeley outpost brings the same reformer-based sculpt format to the East Bay with a class lineup that zeroes in on targeted muscle groups. The SIX PACKS & BACKS class is a favorite among reviewers who want focused core and back work with plenty of instructor energy.

> "First class with Bree-Anna was amazing! She's a great instructor, funny, and challenged us while still being really encouraging."

> "This was my first time trying reformer pilates. There is definitely a learning curve, but the instructor was very encouraging and helped me find the right equipment and posture."

**Rating:** 4.86 (500+ reviews)

## 6. Zen Pilates and Fitness -- Fusion Pilates

Pleasanton's Zen Pilates and Fitness is a standout for members looking for a more restorative, form-focused pilates experience. Reviewers praise the studio as an ideal choice for beginners, those recovering from injuries, and anyone who wants to slow down and perfect their technique.

> "Highly recommend for beginners, those recovering from injuries, those who want to perfect their form, or those looking for a recovery session in between heavy lifting days."

> "Dr. Navarro is amazing and so attentive. I was nervous since it was my first class, but I was welcomed immediately by the instructor and everyone in the class."

**Rating:** 4.61 (72 reviews)

## 7. Crunch Gym San Mateo -- Burn & Firm Pilates

Crunch Gym's San Mateo location rounds out the list with a pilates class that reviewers call both fun and effective. The Burn & Firm Pilates class features varied interval exercises and a high-energy atmosphere that keeps things interesting from start to finish.

> "An amazing class, even for a beginner! Debby was incredibly positive and pushed us to do our best."

**Rating:** 4.72 (74 reviews)
