# The best pilates studios in Denver and Boulder

Denver and Boulder's pilates scene is as dynamic as the Front Range itself. Whether you're drawn to classical reformer work, high-intensity resistance training, or a mindful mat practice, this metro area has a studio for you. We analyzed hundreds of ClassPass ratings and real member reviews to find the studios that consistently earn top marks for instruction, atmosphere, and results. Here are the best pilates studios in Denver and Boulder right now.

Studios were selected based on review volume, average rating, and consistency of positive feedback from the ClassPass community.

pH7 Pilates is one of Denver's highest-rated reformer studios, and the reviews make it easy to see why. With a beautifully lit, calm space and instructors who bring deep classical training, pH7 has earned a near-perfect rating across nearly 200 reviews. Members consistently highlight the personalized attention they receive, along with classes that cater to a wide range of experience levels.

> "I've been coming to this studio for a while now and love it -- the location and space is beautifully lit, calm, and comfortable. The classes are always motivating and a well-rounded full-body experience. The instructor pays close attention to each individual's form and adjusts the class based on how people are feeling that day, all while keeping a steady flow."

> "I enjoyed the quiet pause and the back-to-basics this class offers. The instructions are clear and every move feels good. I hope to stop in whenever I can!"

Pilates Collective in Denver's University Hills neighborhood is earning a reputation as a welcoming, back-to-basics reformer studio. Members love the inclusive atmosphere and the instructors' emphasis on proper form over speed. Reds Pilates Garage brings a cozy, intimate reformer experience to Denver. With only four reformers per class, you're guaranteed personal attention -- something members call out again and again in their reviews. The small class format makes it especially appealing for anyone new to reformer pilates or looking for hands-on guidance.

> "Great first experience taking a reformer class. Very welcoming and fun. I definitely feel like I got a good experience even with it being my first time on the machine. Excited to go back!"

Their focus on mindfulness, form, and collective well-being makes this studio stand out for members who want more than just a physical challenge.
